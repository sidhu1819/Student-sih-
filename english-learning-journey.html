Chapter 1: The Alphabet

The English alphabet has 26 letters. Each letter has a capital (A) and a small (a) form. The five vowels are A, E, I, O, U. All other letters are consonants.

Chapter 2: Nouns

A noun is a word for a person, place, or thing. For example, 'boy', 'school', and 'book' are all nouns.

Person: teacher, Rohan. Place: village, Punjab. Thing: table, ball.

Test Your Knowledge

1. Which word is a noun in the sentence: "The dog ran home."?

Chapter 3: Verbs

A verb is an action word. It tells you what the noun is doing. For example, 'run', 'jump', and 'read' are verbs.

She reads a book. He plays outside.

Test Your Knowledge

1. Which word is the verb in the sentence: "Birds fly in the sky."?

Chapter 4: Simple Sentences

A simple sentence has a noun (subject) and a verb (predicate). It expresses a complete thought.

Subject + Verb. "The sun shines."
